The Evolution Journey

This project began with a personal revelation about exploitation in political organizing and has grown into a comprehensive educational platform for transforming our political ecosystem. Follow the journey that connects individual empowerment to systemic change.

1

The Breaking Point

June 2025: Discovering that my volunteer work with MomsRising was subsidizing a sophisticated political machine while providing poverty wages. Calculating that our $500 annual stipend represented 85-90% below market rates for professional political work.

2

From Extraction to Education

Creating comprehensive political education materials that organizations won't provide. Sharing everything freely because political education is a human right, not a commodity.

3

Seeing the System

Connecting organizational exploitation to neoliberalism—the invisible doctrine that treats everything as a business. Understanding how this system creates scarcity where abundance is possible.

4

Building Alternatives

Documenting the political evolution happening in communities worldwide. Moving beyond partisan gridlock to collaborative problem-solving. Creating frameworks for abundance-based organizing.

About the Project

From Personal Experience to Collective Transformation

The Political Evolution Project began with a moment of painful clarity about exploitation in political organizing. What started as an investigation into one organization's practices revealed patterns that run throughout our political systems—from how we organize campaigns to how we imagine solutions to our biggest challenges.

This project has evolved from exposing problems to building alternatives. We're documenting the transformation of our political ecosystem from rigid partisan competition to collaborative problem-solving, from scarcity thinking to abundance creation, from extraction to empowerment.

Every resource shared, every story told, and every connection made through this project contributes to a larger movement: transforming how we organize for change in the 21st century.

Our Mission

We exist to accelerate the evolution of political organizing from systems of extraction to movements of empowerment, from scarcity competition to abundance collaboration.

Through education, community building, and resource sharing, we're creating the conditions for a new kind of politics—one that multiplies power rather than hoarding it, solves problems rather than exploiting them, and builds the future we need rather than defending the past we've outgrown.

Core Values

Abundance: We share resources freely because building collective capacity strengthens everyone. Knowledge hoarding perpetuates the very systems we're working to transform.

Evolution: We embrace change as natural and necessary. Just as ecosystems evolve, our political systems must adapt to new realities.

Transparency: Clear information about power and money enables informed participation. Confusion serves exploitation; clarity serves empowerment.

Collaboration: The challenges we face require us to work across traditional boundaries. The future belongs to those who can cooperate effectively.

Sustainability: Organizing that burns people out cannot build lasting change. We model the sustainable practices we want to see.

About Carrie Clendening

I'm a West Virginia mother, organizer, and educator who believes that political change shouldn't require accepting exploitation. My journey from MomsRising Hub Leader to founder of this project taught me that recognizing problems is only the first step—we must also build alternatives.

My background includes grassroots organizing, political education curriculum development, and firsthand experience with how progressive organizations can perpetuate the very power imbalances they claim to fight. But more importantly, I've seen what's possible when people have the tools and knowledge to organize authentically.

This project represents my commitment to sharing everything I've learned about building genuine political power. From detailed curricula to practical tools to frameworks for understanding systemic change, these resources are freely given to everyone ready to transform how we organize.
